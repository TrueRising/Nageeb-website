The Importance of Website Accessibility

Inclusive design isn’t just a nice‑to‑have—it’s a legal and ethical imperative that benefits everyone.

Why Accessibility Matters

Accessibility ensures that people with disabilities can perceive, understand, navigate and interact with your website. Beyond compliance with laws and guidelines, accessible design opens your services to a wider audience and improves usability for all visitors.

1. Understand WCAG Principles

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) provide a framework for making web content more accessible. Focus on four principles: perceivable, operable, understandable and robust. Each principle includes success criteria at different conformance levels.

2. Provide Text Alternatives

Use alt text for images, transcripts for videos and captions for audio content. These alternatives ensure that users with visual or auditory impairments can access your content through screen readers or other assistive technologies.

3. Ensure Keyboard Navigation

Many users rely on a keyboard to navigate. Make sure all interactive elements—links, buttons, forms—are reachable and usable via keyboard only. Test tab order to verify a logical flow.

4. Use Sufficient Colour Contrast

Low contrast makes text hard to read. Choose colour combinations that meet minimum contrast ratios and avoid conveying information through colour alone. Provide additional cues like labels or icons.

5. Build for Responsiveness and Flexibility

Responsive design benefits everyone but is especially important for users who magnify pages or use different devices. Use flexible layouts, avoid fixed widths and allow text to resize without breaking the design.

Conclusion

Designing for accessibility is not only the right thing to do—it’s a smart business decision. Accessible websites rank better in search engines and serve more customers. Need help auditing or improving your site’s accessibility? Contact us.
